Indian Butter Chicken

Description

Indian butter chicken, also known as murgh makhani, is a popular dish in Indian cuisine that is rich, creamy, and full of flavor. It consists of tender pieces of chicken that have been marinated in a spiced yogurt mixture, then cooked in a creamy tomato-based sauce.

To make butter chicken, the chicken is first marinated in a mixture of yogurt and spices such as garam masala, cumin, coriander, and turmeric. The marinated chicken is then grilled or baked until tender and lightly charred.

For the sauce, a mixture of tomatoes, onions, garlic, ginger, and spices are simmered until thick and fragrant. The cooked chicken is then added to the sauce along with a generous amount of butter, cream, and kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves). The butter and cream add richness and depth of flavor to the dish, while the kasuri methi adds a slightly sweet and nutty flavor.

Butter chicken is typically served with rice, naan bread, or roti. It's a delicious and comforting dish that is loved by many and has become a popular staple in Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

For the chicken marinade:

  • Boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts
  • Yogurt
  • Garam masala
  • Cumin
  • Coriander
  • Turmeric
  • Cayenne pepper or red chili powder
  • Salt
  • Lemon juice

For the sauce:

  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Garam masala
  • Cumin
  • Coriander
  • Turmeric
  • Paprika
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Heavy cream
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

It's worth noting that there are many variations of the recipe for butter chicken, and some chefs and home cooks may add additional or different ingredients depending on their preferences.

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken: Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces and mix together the marinade ingredients in a bowl. Add the chicken and stir to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight.
  2. Cook the chicken: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Thread the chicken pieces onto skewers and place them on a baking sheet. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until cooked through. Alternatively, you can cook the chicken on a grill or in a pan until lightly browned and cooked through.
  3. Make the sauce: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat butter over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until they are softened and lightly browned. Add the garlic, ginger, and spices and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Add the tomatoes and tomato paste (if using), and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for 10-15 minutes or until the tomatoes are soft and the sauce has thickened.
  4. Blend the sauce: Let the sauce cool slightly and then puree it in a blender or food processor until smooth.
  5. Finish the sauce: Return the pureed sauce to the pot and add the cream and kasuri methi. Simmer for 5-10 minutes or until the sauce has thickened and the flavors have melded together. Add salt to taste.
  6. Add the chicken: Add the cooked chicken to the sauce and simmer for a few minutes until the chicken is heated through and coated with the sauce.
  7. Serve: Serve hot with rice, naan bread, or roti. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired.
