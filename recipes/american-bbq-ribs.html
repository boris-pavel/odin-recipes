American BBQ Ribs

Description

American BBQ ribs are a popular dish in American cuisine that are made by slow-cooking pork ribs over a low heat, and then basting them with a tangy barbecue sauce. The result is tender, juicy meat with a smoky, charred flavor and a sticky, flavorful sauce.

To make BBQ ribs, pork spare ribs are first coated with a dry rub made of spices such as paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, and brown sugar. The ribs are then smoked or slow-cooked over low heat for several hours until they are tender and have a charred exterior. The ribs can be cooked using a smoker, grill, or oven, depending on the method preferred.

While the ribs are cooking, a barbecue sauce is typically prepared. The sauce is a combination of ketchup, vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, brown sugar, mustard, garlic, and other spices. The sauce is brushed onto the ribs during the last hour of cooking to give them a sweet and tangy flavor and a shiny, caramelized glaze.

BBQ ribs are often served with classic American side dishes such as coleslaw, baked beans, mac and cheese, or cornbread. They're a staple at summer barbecues, picnics, and other outdoor gatherings, but can be enjoyed year-round.

Ingredients

  • Pork spare ribs
  • Dry rub (paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, brown sugar, salt, pepper)
  • Barbecue sauce (ketchup, apple cider vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, brown sugar, mustard, garlic, chili powder, cumin)

Note that there are many variations of the dry rub and barbecue sauce, and some chefs and home cooks may add additional or different ingredients depending on their preferences. Some optional ingredients that can be added for extra flavor include cayenne pepper, liquid smoke, or honey.

It's also important to note that there are different types of ribs, such as baby back ribs, St. Louis-style ribs, and beef ribs, and the seasoning and cooking time may vary depending on the type of rib.

Steps

  1. Prepare the ribs: Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs and trim off any excess fat. Pat the ribs dry with paper towels.
  2. Apply the dry rub: In a small bowl, mix together the spices for the dry rub. Rub the mixture all over the ribs, making sure to cover both sides. Let the ribs sit at room temperature for 30 minutes to an hour to allow the flavors to meld.
  3. Preheat the grill or oven: If using a grill, preheat it to low heat (around 225-250°F/110-120°C). If using an oven, preheat it to 275°F (135°C).
  4. Cook the ribs: Place the ribs on the grill or in the oven, bone-side down. Cook for 3-4 hours, or until the meat is tender and has an internal temperature of 195-203°F (90-95°C). If using a grill, you can add wood chips or chunks for a smokier flavor.
  5. Apply the barbecue sauce: During the last hour of cooking, brush the barbecue sauce onto the ribs, making sure to coat both sides. Be careful not to apply the sauce too early, as it can burn and become bitter. You can also reserve some of the sauce to serve on the side.
  6. Finish the ribs: Once the ribs are cooked and the barbecue sauce is caramelized and sticky, remove them from the grill or oven. Let the ribs rest for 10-15 minutes before cutting them into individual portions.
  7. Serve: Serve the ribs with your choice of side dishes, such as coleslaw, baked beans, mac and cheese, or cornbread. Enjoy!
